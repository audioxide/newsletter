July 02021
This is indeed It

A stop-start month here as our lives slowly came out of pandemic-induced hibernation. There are worse problems to have.

And there are worse artist around than Tyler, the Creator. In fact, it feels like there are few better at the moment. His new album CALL ME IF YOU GET LOST was an eclectic delight. The man can do no wrong.

Elsewhere we returned to our insufferable teen days to celebrate the 20th birthday of Is This It by The Strokes. We’re glad to report it’s as cool as ever. And finally we took inspiration from Kanye West’s creative process on Donda.

We continue to chip away at album artwork credits, and for those of you who think things look a little different this month, that’s because we’ve hit a major milestone on a new site feature.

Be well,

André, Fred, and Andrew

Artwork Stories

We added 11 album artwork credits to the database this month (13 if you include new releases). That means a solid third of our reviews now include artwork credits. Progress is slow, but it’s progress all the same. You can see the full list of those added in July here, but the four below were especially fun to learn about. Click them and see.

Who knew that in the time before Photoshop people had no choice but to build giant sardine cans? Or that Dropout Bear was surrounded by 16th century illustrations? Or that the guy who did artwork for The Fall showed it to his psychiatrist afterwards? Ok fine, that last one isn’t surprising at all.

Site Development

A big milestone has been reached on the site development front, and you’re looking at it. After tinkering away off and on for months we’ve finally built our own custom newsletter generator. We put in the date range and a script generates 90% of a newsletter for us using the site API.

This is part of a larger project to bring our monthly newsletters in-house as a kind of archive. Although it’s not quite there yet, we’ve got the shell of one going using SvelteKit:

Fingers crossed that by this time next month we’ll have ironed out the kinks and integrated it with the site proper. Watch this space. As ever, if you’re interested in looking under the hood all our code is publically viewable on GitHub.
