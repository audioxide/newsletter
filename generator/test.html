May 02021

Brighton rocks

Spring is slowly but surely arriving. Whisper it, but maybe we can all have some fun soon.

We've certainly had fun with this month's music (see what we did there?). Gojira kicked things off with Fortitude, a metal album with just the right amount of cheese. That was followed by Bright Green Field, the debut effort from Brighton outfit Squid. Huge promise there with lots to like - a lovely jumble. And finally we had Daddy's Home by St. Vincent, which plays the neat trick of immersing itself in the '70s while forging a character all of its own.

Elsewhere Andrew reflected on Max Richter's Voices 2 project, and we kept chipping away at album artwork credits, adding 17 more to the database. We'll get them all eventually. On the site development front we did some dependency upgrading and took steps towards a more modular workflow.

A month well spent, all in all.

Catch you soon,

André, Fred, and Andrew

Site development

A fair bit of tinkering this month, almost entirely by Andrew. He updated NuxtJS to v2.15, fixed an alignment issue on the navigation bar, and set up a Storybook for the site's components at audioxide.github.io.

It's early days yet but this should be super helpful for developing the site in a more modular way, giving each component the time and attention it deserves. The platform also helps maintain a kind of visual language, which will only get more important as the site continues to evolve.
